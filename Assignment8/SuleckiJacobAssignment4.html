Heathy Living: Jacob Sulecki

My Journey

For a while now I have been on and off self teaching myself how to lift. A lot of people lift for the looks, the strength, the clout, or for competition. Personally none of these have ever stood out to me as interesting. What has is the fact that lifting has a very good effect on your health. Though it is not nearly as good for cardiovascular health. Lifting and getting up and moving in general is good for overall health in this crazy world we live in.

Some Benefits

Mental health has been under the microscope since people started to talk about it more often likely because of the internet. Any sort of exercise is going to release endorphins that make you feel good and put you in a good mindset to have a better day. You can use different lifting techniques in order to achieve different results. You could lose intra-muscular fat in order to appear more lean. And on the other side, heavy lifting paired with a big diet can make you grow huge if that is your goal. Not only does it strengthen your muscles, it will prevent the risk of joint injuries in everyday life because they will get used to micro-stress thus growing stronger each time.

Where to Start

Like I said above, there are so many different ways to train depending on your goals. If you're like me, young and naturally skinny with a fast metabolism and you want to be bigger. You really want to focus on your diet. You should eat way more calories a day than you could possibly burn while working out. With that dies you hant to be lifting really heavy weight for not as many repetitions. I would say sticking to 3 sets of 5-8 reps per exercise is good for growing in mass for mu body type. Now if you were wanting to lose weight, you need to be in a caloric deficit. This means that you need to eat less calories than you burn a day in order to lose body weight and fat. Pairing This with a lifting routine will help you lose intra-muscular fat and have you looking really lean. For this goal I would do lower weight and higher reps per exercise. Also I would pair exercise so that I'm doing one after the other so that it is mor intense.
